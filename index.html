We asked 100 disaster restoration workers primarily based in Florida and Louisiana if they had been exposed to asbestos, lead and mold while working to rebuild after natural disasters.

Here’s what they shared with us:

In the conducted survey, a total of 100 respondents participated. The gender distribution among the respondents was significantly skewed, with 89 respondents identifying as male and 11 (11%) identifying as female.

Regarding age groups, the respondents spanned a wide range of demographics. The majority of respondents fell into the 30-39 (28%) and 40-49 (31%) age groups, indicating a significant middle-aged demographic in the survey. The 50-59 age group represented 20% of respondents, while the 20-29 and 60-69 age groups accounted for 8% and 12% of respondents, respectively. Only one respondent (1%) was in the 70-79 age group.

This data suggests that the survey sample is predominantly male, with a substantial representation of individuals between the ages of 30 and 49. This information can be valuable for further analysis and interpretation of your research findings, depending on the research objectives and questions you are addressing.

72 of the 100 workers said they have been exposed to mold.

52 said they have been exposed to asbestos.

48 said they have been exposed to lead.

Combined, 80 workers said they had been exposed to at least one toxin while doing disaster restoration work.

28 workers said they didn’t know if they had been exposed.
